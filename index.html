EHS data accumulates. It rarely connects. I work on the gap — between field operations, information systems, and the data science that should link them.

Ten years implementing integrated management systems on-site. Five years as product lead for EHS SaaS platforms. Graduate work in management information systems and industrial environment. The writing is here.

On the field

EHS software fails because it's specced by people who've never investigated a fatality. The data model reflects what's easy to capture, not what a root-cause analysis needs.

Most EHS KPIs measure what already went wrong. Leading indicators require a different data architecture — one most organisations haven't designed.

The best integrated management system becomes invisible. Compliance stops being a burden and starts being a byproduct of how people naturally work. That's an information design problem, not a training one.

Regulatory change isn't a risk to manage. It's a roadmap signal. Read it the way an analyst reads an earnings call.

"Digital transformation" projects fail when the tool is bought before the data model is designed. Most EHS implementations are bought first.

Safety culture isn't a training problem. It's an information design problem. People behave differently when the feedback loop is tight and the signal is clear.
